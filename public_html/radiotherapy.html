RADIOTHERAPY
  • In radiotherapy, small doses of radiation are given to kill the cancer cells around the breast.
  • Radiotherapy usually starts 6 weeks after surgery when the wound has healed.
  • If you have been advised to undergo chemotherapy and radiotherapy, the chemotherapy will often be given first.

Common side effects of radiotherapy include:

  • the skin may become red, itchy, sore or tender.
  • tiredness
  • hair loss in the treated area but not the hair on the head.
  • scarring of the lung
  • numbness, weakness and pain of the shoulder, arm and hand
  • lymphoedema
  • the breast may shrink over time
